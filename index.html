Is this home your (or a relation's) primary place of residence? ?
A primary place of residence is where you live on a permanent basis. You can only have one primary residence.

Vacation cottages, investment properties, and rental homes do not qualify. If renting, see CRA Guide RC4231.
🚫
Not eligible for this rebate. The home must be your or a relation's primary place of residence. If renting, see CRA Guide RC4231.
Are you a first-time home buyer? (Required for Bill C-4) ?
Bill C-4 first-time buyer definition: You must not have owned AND occupied any home as your principal residence anywhere in the world at any time in the 4 calendar years before your purchase year.

If buying with a spouse or partner, both of you must independently meet this rule.

You must also be a Canadian citizen or permanent resident, and at least 18 years old.

Work permit and study permit holders do not qualify.
📋
The Nova Scotia First-Time Home Buyers Rebate is a separate provincial program — claim it with Service Nova Scotia & Municipal Relations, not through the CRA. Visit novascotia.ca for eligibility.
